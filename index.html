The science

The medication that finally turns down the noise.

GLP-1 receptor agonists are a class of prescription medication originally developed for type-2 diabetes. They work with your body’s own appetite hormones — slowing how fast your stomach empties, calming the constant food chatter in your head, and helping you feel full on less.

The result, in clinical trials and in real Indian patients: sustained weight loss without crash diets or willpower wars.

  • Once-weekly injection — not a daily pill
  • Compounded in GMP-licensed Indian pharmacies
  • Dose titrated up gradually to minimise side effects
  • Combined with coaching, sleep, and protein-led meals
Most prescribed

Semaglutide

Compounded · Once weekly

Most studied GLP-1 receptor agonist. Suitable for most adults seeking 10–15% body-weight reduction over 6–12 months.

  • Weeks 1–4: 0.25 mg titration
  • Weeks 5–8: 0.5 mg maintenance
  • Beyond: titrated to response
Strongest results

Tirzepatide

Compounded · Once weekly

Newer dual-action GIP/GLP-1. For adults seeking 15–22% reduction or who haven’t responded to GLP-1 alone.

  • Greater appetite suppression
  • Improved insulin sensitivity
  • Higher monthly investment

Compounded medications are not standard pharmaceutical products and are produced in licensed pharmacies under physician supervision. Suitability is determined by your prescribing doctor based on your individual medical history. Information here is educational, not medical advice.

Ask a doctor
Is GLP-1 medication safe for Indians?

Yes, when prescribed and monitored by a licensed doctor. GLP-1 agonists like semaglutide and tirzepatide have been studied in tens of thousands of patients worldwide, including Indian cohorts. The most common side effects are mild nausea, slow digestion, and fatigue, especially in the first few weeks. Your doctor titrates the dose gradually to minimise these and stays available throughout.

Will I gain the weight back if I stop?

If you stop GLP-1 abruptly without behaviour changes, partial regain is common — that’s why we pair medication with meals, sleep, and movement coaching from day one. Most patients taper down rather than stop cold, and many maintain their results long after.

How is this different from going to a clinic?

You see the same calibre of doctor — but on your phone, on your schedule, with continuous chat support between visits. No waiting rooms. No travel. Cold-chain medication arrives at your door, and dosage adjusts based on your weekly check-ins.

Is GLP-1 legal and prescribed in India?

Yes. GLP-1 receptor agonists are prescription medications regulated by CDSCO and routinely prescribed by Indian endocrinologists. Compounded variants are produced in GMP-licensed Indian pharmacies under physician supervision.

Do you ship across India?

We currently serve 18 metros and tier-1 cities with cold-chain logistics. Expansion to tier-2 in 2026. Enter your PIN code at checkout to confirm coverage.

What if the medication doesn’t work for me?

If after 60 days of consistent use you’re not seeing measurable progress, we refund your first month — no questions, no clauses. Your doctor will also reassess fit, dose, and whether a different molecule is appropriate.

How does the doctor consult work?

After your assessment, a licensed Indian doctor reviews your case asynchronously (usually within 24 hours). If you qualify, they prescribe and message you directly. Follow-ups can be chat or video, your choice — included in your plan.

Can I cancel anytime?

Yes — pause or cancel from your dashboard at any time. No exit fees. If you’ve been billed for a shipment that hasn’t left our pharmacy, we refund it.
